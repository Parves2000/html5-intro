Visited: Mount Fuji Kawaguchiko on July 2025

What's the best way to get there?

1. By Train (to Kawaguchiko) ⏱ Approx: 2.5 hours | 💴 Cost: ~2,500–4,000 yen.
🔹 Option A: Limited Express from Shinjuku Take the JR Limited Express "Fuji Excursion" from Shinjuku Station directly to Kawaguchiko Station 🕐 1 hr 50 mins 💴 ~4,130 yen (reserved seat)
🔹 Option B: Regular Route (cheaper) Take a JR Chuo Line to Otsuki Station Transfer to Fujikyu Railway to Kawaguchiko 🕐 About 2.5 hours 💴 ~2,640 yen.

2. By Highway Bus ⏱ Approx: 2 hours | 💴 Cost: ~2,000–2,500 yen
🔹 Option A:Direct buses from Shinjuku, Tokyo Station, or Shibuya to Kawaguchiko Station, Fuji-Q Highland, or 5th Station Operated by Fujikyu.
🔹 Option B:Keio Bus Cheaper and comfortable with luggage storage 🕐 1 hr 45 mins – 2.5 hrs 💴 ~2,000 yen one-way.
📲 Advance reservation recommended, especially in peak season. Search for “Highway Bus Kawaguchiko.”

When is the best time to visit there?

📅 October to February (especially December–January)
❄️ Winter months offer the clearest views – cold air = less haze
🗻 Mount Fuji is beautifully snow-capped
🌅 Best chance for stunning sunrise/sunset shots
💡 Tip: Go early in the morning (6–9 AM) — clouds often cover Fuji by midday.

What's good to eat there?

Yoshida no Udon (吉田のうどん) Very chewy, thick udon noodles Topped with cabbage, horse meat (optional), miso/soy soup, and spicy pepper Local to Fujiyoshida City near Mt. Fuji’s 5th station trail.
Where to eat: 🔹 Local family-run udon shops around Fujiyoshida (like Miyaki Udon or Idaten Udon)

Fujisan Soft Cream Mount Fuji–shaped or blue-colored ice cream Some are flavored with local ingredients like blueberry, lavender, or wasabi

Fruits & Wine (in season) Yamanashi is famous for:

  • 🍇 Grapes
  • 🍑 Peaches
  • 🍷 Local wine (Yamanashi is Japan’s wine capital)
Visit fruit-picking farms (July–Sept) or wineries near Katsunuma!
